# Plumbing — Entrance Quiz

Instructions: Read each question carefully. Choose the single best answer. No outside resources, no phones.

1. What is the minimum slope required for a 3-inch horizontal drain line under the IPC?
   A. 1/8 inch per foot
   B. 1/4 inch per foot
   C. 1/2 inch per foot
   D. 1 inch per foot

   A. Notify the homeowner and continue work
   B. Locate and shut off the appropriate isolation valve and relieve pressure

   A. The main shutoff valve near the meter
   B. The T&P relief valve outlet
   C. The end of a quick-closing valve supply branch

4. Which pipe material requires dielectric unions when joining to galvanized steel?
   A. CPVC
   B. PEX
   C. Copper
   D. ABS

   B. A location where discharge will not create a burn or slip hazard

6. What is the maximum trap arm length (horizontal distance from trap weir to vent) for a 1-1/2-inch fixture drain?
   A. 2 feet
   B. 3.5 feet
   C. 5 feet
   D. 8 feet

7. When pressure-testing a new DWV rough-in with water, the system should hold a minimum water column of:
   A. 5 feet for 15 minutes
   B. 10 feet for 15 minutes
   C. 10 feet for at least 5 minutes with no drop
   D. Any positive head for 30 minutes

8. A plumber is sweating copper fittings inside a wall cavity. Which PPE item is most critical beyond safety glasses?
   A. Hard hat
   B. Fire-resistant gloves and a heat shield behind the pipe

9. Which venting method uses a single vent pipe that serves both as a drain and a vent without a separate vent stack?
   A. Air admittance valve
   B. Wet vent
   C. Common vent
   D. Island vent

10. PEX tubing should NOT be used in which of the following applications without special consideration?
    A. In-slab radiant heat loops
    B. Hot water supply in a residential bathroom
    C. Direct UV (sunlight) exposure over long runs

11. What is the primary purpose of a cleanout installed at the base of a soil stack?

    B. To provide access for rodding obstructions from the stack through the building drain

    A. 3 feet
    B. 5 feet
    C. 8 feet

    A. Water hammer when the hose valve closes quickly
    B. Contaminants from siphoning back into the potable supply

    A. Replace the P-trap immediately
