# Finish Carpentry & Trim — Entrance Quiz

**Questions:** 20  |  **Passing score:** 16/20 (80%)  |  **Time limit:** 30 minutes

Instructions: Read each question carefully. Choose the single best answer. No outside resources, no phones.

---

1. When installing baseboard on an inside corner, the preferred professional method is to:
   A. Miter both pieces at 45° and glue the joint
   B. Cope one piece to the profile of the other, butting the first piece square into the corner
   C. Butt both pieces square into the corner and fill the gap with caulk
   D. Back-miter both pieces at 22.5° and nail them together

2. A scarf joint on a long baseboard run should be located:
   A. At the midpoint between two studs to avoid nail interference
   B. Over a stud, with both pieces nailed through the joint into the framing
   C. At any convenient location and secured with construction adhesive only
   D. At door openings so the joint is hidden by the casing

3. The standard reveal (setback) of door casing from the inside edge of the door jamb is typically:
   A. ⅛ inch
   B. ¼ inch
   C. ½ inch
   D. ¾ inch

4. Which nail size is most appropriate for attaching 3½-inch tall baseboard to wood studs through ½-inch drywall?
   A. 4d (1½-inch) finish nail
   B. 6d (2-inch) finish nail
   C. 15-gauge 2½-inch finish nail
   D. 16-gauge 1¼-inch brad nail

5. The IRC requires a minimum stair tread depth (nosing to nosing) of:
   A. 8 inches
   B. 9 inches
   C. 10 inches
   D. 11 inches

6. When cutting an outside miter on casing for a door frame that is not perfectly plumb or square, the carpenter should:
   A. Assume 45° and force the joint closed with nails
   B. Use a bevel gauge to find the actual angle and bisect it for each piece
   C. Cut both pieces at 45° and fill the gap with wood filler
   D. Cope one side of the outside corner to match the profile

7. Door undercuts are required primarily to:
   A. Make the door easier to open and close
   B. Allow return air to flow from closed rooms back to the HVAC system
   C. Provide clearance for carpet and pad installation
   D. Prevent moisture from wicking into the bottom of the door

8. What is the purpose of back-cutting (back-beveling) a cope joint?
   A. To add glue surface area to the joint
   B. To ensure only the face profile contacts the adjoining piece, improving fit and avoiding rocking
   C. To reduce the thickness of the trim so it sits flat against the drywall
   D. To allow the joint to expand and contract with humidity changes

9. A finish carpenter uses a jamb extension on an interior door. When is a jamb extension necessary?
   A. When the door is taller than 7 feet
   B. When the wall thickness is greater than the depth of the pre-hung door jamb
   C. When the door will receive a double layer of casing
   D. When the door is installed in a load-bearing wall

10. When scribing a piece of trim to an uneven wall or floor, the scribe tool should be set to:
    A. Half the widest gap between the trim and the surface
    B. The full width of the widest gap between the trim and the surface
    C. 1/16 inch, regardless of the gap size
    D. The thickness of the trim stock being scribed

11. Which personal protective equipment is required when operating a miter saw in an enclosed space?
    A. Face shield only; hearing protection is not needed for short cuts
    B. Safety glasses or goggles and hearing protection at minimum
    C. A full-face respirator for all interior cutting operations
    D. Safety glasses only; no hearing protection is required below 8-hour exposure

12. Stair riser height, per the IRC, must not exceed:
    A. 6¾ inches
    B. 7¾ inches
    C. 8½ inches
    D. 9 inches

13. When installing window stool (the flat interior sill piece), the stool must:
    A. Be cut perfectly square at both ends and nailed flush to the wall
    B. Project past the casing and have "horns" that return to the wall on each side
    C. Be installed after the side casings so it overlaps them
    D. Be shimmed to be perfectly level regardless of window rough opening slope

14. A long run of crown molding needs a splice in the middle of a wall. The correct joint is:
    A. A square butt joint glued and pinned
    B. A 45° scarf joint, oriented so the overlapping piece faces away from the primary viewing direction
    C. A coped joint at each end of both pieces
    D. A biscuit joint reinforced with corner brackets

15. Which nail gun is most appropriate for attaching crown molding to wall and ceiling framing?
    A. A 21-degree framing nailer with 3-inch nails
    B. A 15-gauge finish nailer with 2 to 2½-inch nails
    C. A 23-gauge pin nailer with ¾-inch pins
    D. A roofing coil nailer with 1¼-inch nails

16. When installing hardwood stair treads, the maximum variation in individual riser heights within a single stairway run, per the IRC, is:
    A. ¼ inch
    B. ⅜ inch
    C. ½ inch
    D. ¾ inch

17. A carpenter is installing chair rail and notices the walls have a slight bow. The best approach to maintain a level, consistent installation is to:
    A. Follow the wall contour exactly, letting the rail bow with the wall
    B. Strike a level chalk line and scribe/shim the rail to the line, shimming behind as needed
    C. Install furring strips to build out the low spots before attaching the rail
    D. Use a thicker profile rail that spans the bow without shimming

18. What is the standard reveal between the door stop and the face of the door casing on a pre-hung door unit?
    A. No reveal; the casing is flush to the stop
    B. ⅛ inch
    C. ¼ inch
    D. ⅜ inch

19. When face-nailing solid hardwood baseboard to avoid visible nail holes, the preferred technique is to:
    A. Use a pin nailer with 23-gauge pins that leave nearly invisible holes
    B. Drive finish nails at a slight downward angle at the bottom of the baseboard into the plate
    C. Use construction adhesive only and avoid any nails
    D. Countersink screws and cover with wood putty

20. A journeyman is installing casing around a door in a 105-year-old house and notices the jamb is out of plumb by ½ inch over its height. The correct approach is to:
    A. Shim the jamb plumb before installing any casing
    B. Scribe the casing reveal line to the jamb's actual position and cut the miters to the bisected angle
    C. Install casing at standard 45° miters and fill corner gaps with caulk
    D. Use flexible casing profile that bends to follow the out-of-plumb jamb
