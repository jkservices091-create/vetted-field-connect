# Roofing — Entrance Quiz

**Questions:** 20  |  **Passing score:** 16/20 (80%)  |  **Time limit:** 30 minutes

Instructions: Read each question carefully. Choose the single best answer. No outside resources, no phones.

---

1. Per OSHA fall protection rules, residential roofers must have fall protection when working at a height of:
   A. 4 feet or more above a lower level
   B. 6 feet or more above a lower level
   C. 10 feet or more above a lower level
   D. Only when the roof pitch exceeds 6:12

   A. 12 inches from the eave edge
   B. 24 inches inside the exterior wall line
   C. To the ridge
   D. 18 inches from the eave edge

3. Which type of underlayment is considered a secondary water-resistive barrier and is required under most asphalt shingle installations?
   A. 6-mil polyethylene sheeting
   B. Felt underlayment (No. 15 or No. 30 felt) or a synthetic equivalent
   C. Rosin paper
   D. Housewrap

4. Standard three-tab asphalt shingles are typically installed with what exposure (reveal)?
   A. 4 inches
   B. 5 inches
   C. 6 inches
   D. 7 inches

5. When nailing asphalt shingles, nails must be placed:
   A. Through the top of the shingle tabs
   B. In the nailing zone (manufacturer's nail line), above the adhesive strip
   C. Just below the adhesive strip so the nail head is hidden by the upper course
   D. At the centerline of each tab

6. A step flashing is used at which location?
   A. Along the ridge to seal the peak
   B. At the junction of a sloped roof and a vertical wall (such as a dormer or chimney side)
   C. In open valleys to direct water toward the center
   D. At the eave edge to seal between the shingles and the fascia

7. What is the primary purpose of a kickout flashing at the bottom of a wall-roof intersection?
   A. To secure the bottom course of step flashing to the wall
   B. To divert water away from the wall and into the gutter, preventing water intrusion behind the siding
   C. To provide a starter course for the step flashing sequence
   D. To seal the gap between the gutter and the fascia board

8. Proper attic ventilation requires a balance between:
   A. Ridge vents and power attic ventilators
   B. Intake ventilation at the soffits and exhaust ventilation at or near the ridge
   C. Gable-end vents only, evenly spaced
   D. A single large exhaust fan and no intake vents

9. A woven (laced) valley is installed by:
   A. Running metal flashing down the valley center and trimming shingles 6 inches back on each side
   B. Alternating courses of shingles from each roof plane across the valley, weaving them together
   C. Cutting shingles diagonally and installing them along one side only
   D. Applying self-adhered membrane over the valley and then capping with ridge shingles

10. When setting up a straight ladder for roof access, the base should be set out from the wall a distance equal to:
    A. 1 foot for every 3 feet of height
    B. 1 foot for every 4 feet of height
    C. 1 foot for every 2 feet of height
    D. At least 3 feet regardless of height

11. Which of the following best describes a "closed-cut valley"?
    A. The valley is covered entirely by shingles from one plane, while the other plane's shingles are trimmed back 2 inches from the valley centerline
    B. Metal valley flashing is fully concealed under the shingles on both sides
    C. Shingles from both planes are woven together across the valley
    D. A continuous strip of ridge cap shingles is applied down the valley

12. What is the minimum head lap for standard felt underlayment on a roof pitch of 4:12 or greater?
    A. 2 inches
    B. 4 inches
    C. 6 inches
    D. 12 inches

13. Before starting a tear-off on a reroof, a worker should:
    A. Begin at the ridge and work down to avoid injuring workers below
    B. Begin at the eave and work up
    C. Strip the entire roof before inspecting the deck

14. Which nail is the correct fastener for most residential asphalt shingle applications per manufacturer instructions?
    A. 1-inch roofing nail with a 3/8-inch head
    B. 1-1/4 inch roofing nail with a 3/8-inch head, minimum

    A. Nail through the soft area with extra nails to stiffen it
    B. Apply a layer of 1/4-inch plywood overlay and proceed

    B. Whenever a worker is on a roof, regardless of pitch

    A. Over the underlayment so water sheds onto the drip edge
