# HVAC — Entrance Quiz

Instructions: Read each question carefully. Choose the single best answer. No outside resources, no phones.

   A. OSHA 10 card
   B. EPA 608 technician certification (Type I, II, III, or Universal)
   C. NATE certification at the journeyman level
   D. A state mechanical contractor license only

   A. The correct refrigerant charge for a new system
   B. The proper equipment sizing (heating and cooling capacity) for a specific building

   A. 1/8 inch per foot

   C. 1/2 inch per foot

6. A condensate trap on a drain leaving a negative-pressure air handler is installed to:
   A. Increase condensate flow velocity through the drain line
   B. Prevent air from being pulled back into the air handler through the open drain

7. When installing refrigerant suction line insulation, the primary reason is to:
   A. Protect the line from physical damage
   B. Prevent condensation and heat gain on the low-pressure, low-temperature suction line

   D. Prevent vibration noise transmission through the structure

8. Residential gas furnaces are classified into venting categories based on flue gas temperature and pressure. A Category IV furnace requires:
   A. A Type B double-wall metal vent

   C. A sealed, positive-pressure, corrosion-resistant vent system (typically PVC or CPVC)

9. Combustion air for a fuel-burning appliance in a confined space may be supplied by:
   A. Opening the nearest exterior window before each heating cycle
   B. Two permanent openings communicating with adjoining interior spaces or directly to the outdoors
   C. A single large return-air grille at the ceiling of the mechanical room

10. A high MERV filter (MERV 13+) installed in a residential system without verifying blower capacity can cause:
    A. Increased refrigerant charge loss through the filter media
    B. Reduced airflow due to high filter resistance, potentially freezing the coil or overheating the heat exchanger

    A. The compressor nameplate rating
    B. The air handler or furnace blower's rated maximum external static pressure

    B. A bedroom with the door closed
