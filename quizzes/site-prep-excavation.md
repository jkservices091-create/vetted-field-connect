# Site Prep & Excavation — Entrance Quiz

**Questions:** 20  |  **Passing score:** 16/20 (80%)  |  **Time limit:** 30 minutes

Instructions: Read each question carefully. Choose the single best answer. No outside resources, no phones.

---

1. Before any excavation begins on a job site, what is the required first step under federal law?
   A. Obtain a building permit from the local authority
   B. Call 811 to request utility locates
   C. Install erosion-control silt fencing around the perimeter
   D. Conduct a soil classification test

2. A trench is 6 feet deep and dug in Type B soil. Which protective system is acceptable per OSHA excavation standards?
   A. No protection needed for trenches under 8 feet
   B. Sloping the walls at a 1:1 (45-degree) angle
   C. Sloping the walls at a ¾:1 angle
   D. A single row of timber sheeting without bracing

3. When must a competent person inspect an excavation site?
   A. Once at the start of the project and after any rainfall over 1 inch
   B. Only when the trench exceeds 10 feet in depth
   C. Daily before work begins and after any event that could affect conditions
   D. Weekly, or whenever a new crew arrives on site

4. Which soil type is considered the most stable and can be sloped at the steepest angle?
   A. Type C
   B. Type B
   C. Type A
   D. Granular (sandy) soil

5. Spoils from an excavation must be placed at a minimum of how far from the edge of the trench?
   A. 1 foot
   B. 2 feet
   C. 4 feet
   D. 6 feet

6. A worker is in a 5-foot-deep trench when water begins seeping in through the walls. What is the correct immediate action?
   A. Continue working while a pump is set up to remove water
   B. Exit the trench immediately and notify the competent person
   C. Install a sheet-pile wall before exiting
   D. Lay sandbags along the trench walls to slow the seepage

7. Which of the following best describes a "benchmark" in site preparation?
   A. A temporary structure used to support formwork
   B. A permanent or semi-permanent reference point used to establish elevations on site
   C. The minimum compaction percentage required by the geotechnical report
   D. The property corner marker set by the surveyor

8. What does a laser level or optical level primarily help establish during rough grading?
   A. Soil classification type
   B. Bearing capacity of subgrade
   C. Consistent elevation control across the site
   D. Location of underground utilities

9. Silt fence must be installed so that the fabric is:
   A. Stretched tightly above grade with no buried edge
   B. Partially buried in a trench and backfilled to anchor it
   C. Staked only at corners, with slack between posts
   D. Placed parallel to the slope, not across it

10. When grading around a foundation, the finish grade should slope away from the structure at a minimum of:
    A. ½ inch per foot for at least 6 feet
    B. 6 inches over the first 10 feet
    C. 2% grade for 20 feet
    D. 1 inch per foot for at least 10 feet

11. A hydraulic excavator operator is preparing to swing the boom while workers are nearby. What is the safest procedure?
    A. Honk the horn once and continue the swing
    B. Establish a swing radius exclusion zone and ensure no workers enter it
    C. Reduce swing speed and allow workers to pass through quickly
    D. Post a spotter only when swinging toward the street side

12. Which of the following describes Type C soil?
    A. Intact rock or cemented soil that can be cored without crumbling
    B. Previously disturbed soil, granular soil, or soil with water seeping in
    C. Cohesive soil with an unconfined compressive strength of 1.5 tsf or greater
    D. Fissured soil with a compressive strength between 0.5 and 1.5 tsf

13. When using a trench box (shield) system, workers must stay:
    A. Anywhere inside the shield; the box provides full protection along its entire length
    B. Within the shield area, not in the unprotected ends
    C. At least 2 feet from the shield walls at all times
    D. Outside the trench unless the box extends 18 inches above grade

14. A contractor needs to determine where to set a building pad. Which instrument is most appropriate for establishing a precise elevation reference?
    A. A 4-foot hand level
    B. A builder's level or total station
    C. A tape measure and line level
    D. A water level hose

15. What is the primary purpose of a dewatering system during excavation?
    A. To moisten dry soil before compaction
    B. To lower the groundwater table so excavation can proceed safely and in dry conditions
    C. To dispose of surface runoff into the municipal storm system
    D. To mix bentonite slurry into the trench walls for stabilization

16. Before operating a skid-steer or compact track loader on a slope, the operator should:
    A. Always travel straight up or straight down the slope, never across it
    B. Attach a drag chain to slow descent
    C. Keep the bucket raised for counterbalance on steep grades
    D. Reduce tire pressure to increase traction

17. After a significant rain event, who is authorized to declare a trench safe to re-enter?
    A. The crew foreman, after a visual check
    B. Any experienced journeyman who was on site before the rain
    C. The competent person, after inspection
    D. The general contractor's superintendent by phone approval

18. Erosion-control wattles (straw wattles) are typically installed:
    A. At the top of slopes to divert runoff away from the excavation
    B. Along the contour of slopes to slow and filter sheet flow
    C. Inside the trench to absorb groundwater
    D. Around the perimeter of the building pad as a permanent drainage feature

19. A site plan shows a finish floor elevation (FFE) of 104.50. The existing grade at the building corner is 102.80. How much fill is needed at that corner (ignoring footing depth)?
    A. 1.30 feet
    B. 1.70 feet
    C. 2.50 feet
    D. 0.80 feet

20. When excavating near an existing structure's footing, the safest practice is to:
    A. Excavate quickly so the footing is unsupported for the shortest possible time
    B. Determine the footing depth first and avoid excavating below the 45-degree bearing plane from the footing base
    C. Shore the new trench walls only after reaching footing depth
    D. Keep excavation at least 10 feet horizontal distance regardless of depth
