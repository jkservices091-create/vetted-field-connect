# Painting — Entrance Quiz

**Questions:** 20  |  **Passing score:** 16/20 (80%)  |  **Time limit:** 30 minutes

Instructions: Read each question carefully. Choose the single best answer. No outside resources, no phones.

---

1. Under EPA's Renovation, Repair and Painting (RRP) rule, lead-safe work practices are required in pre-1978 homes when a painted surface disturbed is larger than:
   A. 1 square foot on interior surfaces or 6 square feet on exterior surfaces
   B. 2 square feet on any surface
   C. 6 square feet on interior surfaces or 20 square feet on exterior surfaces
   D. Any size if the home was built before 1978

2. Before painting over a glossy alkyd surface with latex paint, the correct surface prep step is to:
   A. Apply a coat of oil-based primer and skip sanding
   B. Lightly sand or chemically degloss the surface to improve adhesion
   C. Wipe with mineral spirits and paint immediately
   D. Apply two coats of latex paint directly; modern latex adheres without prep

3. Which primer type is most effective for blocking heavy tannin bleed-through on new cedar or redwood siding?
   A. Water-based latex PVA primer
   B. 100% acrylic exterior primer
   C. Oil-based alkyd primer or shellac-based primer
   D. Water-based drywall primer-sealer

4. A painter is rolling a ceiling in a poorly ventilated room using a solvent-based paint. Which respiratory protection is appropriate?
   A. An N95 particulate respirator
   B. An organic-vapor cartridge respirator (half-face or better)
   C. A dust mask is sufficient since the room has a window cracked
   D. No respiratory protection is needed for oil-based paint under 1 gallon

5. Which paint sheen is best suited for a high-humidity bathroom where scrubability and mildew resistance are priorities?
   A. Flat
   B. Matte
   C. Eggshell
   D. Semi-gloss

6. The minimum recoat time between coats of a standard water-based latex paint under normal conditions (77°F, 50% RH) is typically:
   A. 15–30 minutes
   B. 1–2 hours
   C. 4–6 hours
   D. 24 hours

7. When setting up a work area in a pre-1978 home under RRP rules, interior containment requires:
   A. Posting a warning sign on the exterior of the building
   B. Covering the floor and any furniture within the work area with plastic sheeting taped to the walls
   C. Wearing a full hazmat suit regardless of the size of the disturbance
   D. Notifying the local building department 5 days in advance

8. A surface shows white, crystalline deposits on a masonry exterior wall before repainting. The correct remediation is to:
   A. Prime over the deposits immediately with an alkali-resistant primer
   B. Remove efflorescence by wire brushing and treating with a dilute acid wash, then prime after drying
   C. Power wash with plain water and apply two coats of paint while still damp
   D. Skim-coat the deposits with mortar and paint when dry

9. You are spray painting with an airless sprayer and notice a tailing pattern (elongated ends) instead of a clean elliptical fan. The most likely cause is:
   A. The tip size is too small for the material
   B. The spray pressure is too low or the tip is worn
   C. The paint has been thinned too much
   D. The sprayer fan width adjustment is fully open

10. When painting adjacent to a wet surface (cutting in next to a freshly rolled section), the best practice to avoid lap marks is to:
    A. Wait for the rolled section to fully dry before cutting in
    B. Maintain a wet edge by working quickly and overlapping into the wet section
    C. Apply a heavier coat when cutting in to match the rolled texture
    D. Use a different sheen for cut-in areas than for rolled areas

11. Drop cloths should be used on all floor surfaces in a paint work area. Which type provides the best slip resistance and paint absorption?
    A. Plastic poly sheeting (6 mil)
    B. Rosin paper
    C. Canvas drop cloths
    D. Kraft paper taped flat to the floor

12. A painter is working from a stepladder and needs to reach 6 inches beyond comfortable arm's reach to one side. The correct action is to:
    A. Lean and extend as far as possible while keeping one foot on the ladder
    B. Descend and reposition the ladder
    C. Have a coworker hold the base of the ladder while reaching
    D. Use the top cap of the stepladder as a standing surface for better reach

13. Shellac-based primer (such as BIN) is the preferred choice for which substrate condition?
    A. New uncoated drywall before latex topcoat
    B. Severe water stains, smoke odor, or heavy bleed-through that oil primer cannot fully block
    C. Exterior wood that will receive a solid-color stain
    D. Galvanized metal before oil-based topcoat

14. After repainting a wall with the same color, a painter notices the repainted area looks lighter than the surrounding wall in certain lighting. The most likely cause is:
    A. The new batch of paint was a different sheen
    B. Flashing caused by uneven porosity — the patched/primed areas absorbed paint differently
    C. The paint was applied too thickly in the repainted area
    D. The roller nap was too short for the surface texture

15. Black mildew stains are visible on a bathroom ceiling before repainting. The correct sequence is:
    A. Apply two coats of mildew-resistant paint directly over the stains
    B. Clean with a bleach solution, allow to dry fully, spot-prime the stains, then apply finish coat
    C. Sand the stains smooth, prime, and topcoat
    D. Cover with an oil-based primer and proceed without cleaning

16. Which ladder safety rule applies when using an extension ladder to access a wall for painting?
    A. The base of the ladder should be placed so the angle is approximately 60° from horizontal
    B. The ladder should extend at least 3 feet above the roofline or working surface
    C. The feet should be placed on a drop cloth to protect the floor
    D. Both rails must be secured to the wall with rope ties before climbing

17. Paint applied to an exterior wood surface that is too wet will most likely result in:
    A. Faster drying and a smoother finish
    B. Blistering or peeling as trapped moisture vapor pushes through the film
    C. Deeper penetration of the primer and improved adhesion
    D. A slight darkening of the wood color only

18. When painting over a previously painted surface with a known color change (dark to light), the most efficient professional approach is to:
    A. Apply 4 or more coats of finish paint to achieve opacity
    B. Apply a hide-optimized primer or tinted primer before the finish coat
    C. Use a gloss finish coat, which has better hide than flat
    D. Sand down to bare substrate before applying finish paint

19. Which ventilation requirement applies to interior painting with solvent-based coatings?
    A. Open a window at least 2 inches; no mechanical ventilation is needed
    B. Provide continuous fresh-air ventilation sufficient to keep solvent vapors below 10% of the LEL, and prohibit ignition sources
    C. Run the HVAC system on recirculate to dilute vapors throughout the building
    D. Ventilation is only required for spray application, not brush or roller

20. A painter is about to apply a latex topcoat and the temperature forecast shows the overnight low at 42°F within 4 hours of application. The correct action is to:
    A. Proceed; latex paint can cure at any temperature above freezing
    B. Thin the paint with 10% water to lower the freeze point
    C. Postpone painting; most latex paints require temperatures to stay above 50°F during application and through the cure period
    D. Switch to an alkyd paint with no minimum temperature restriction
