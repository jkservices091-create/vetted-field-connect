# Electrical — Entrance Quiz

**Questions:** 20  |  **Passing score:** 16/20 (80%)  |  **Time limit:** 30 minutes

Instructions: Read each question carefully. Choose the single best answer. No outside resources, no phones.

---

1. Before working on a circuit, an electrician performs lockout/tagout. The correct sequence is:
   A. Turn off the breaker, apply the lock, test for voltage, tag the panel
   B. Identify the energy source, shut it off, lock it out, tag it, and verify zero energy with a meter
   C. Tag the panel first, then turn off the breaker and test
   D. Apply the lock at the panel, then have the homeowner verify the outlet is dead

2. Which wire gauge is rated for a 20-ampere branch circuit using copper conductors?
   A. 14 AWG
   B. 10 AWG
   C. 12 AWG
   D. 8 AWG

3. GFCI protection is required by the NEC in all of the following locations EXCEPT:
   A. Bathroom receptacles
   B. Garage receptacles
   C. Receptacles in a dedicated home office interior room with no water sources
   D. Outdoor receptacles

4. A kitchen requires at least how many 20-ampere small-appliance circuits for countertop receptacles?
   A. One
   B. Two
   C. Three
   D. Four

5. Tamper-resistant (TR) receptacles are required by the NEC in:
   A. Kitchens only
   B. All 15- and 20-ampere receptacles in dwelling units
   C. Bedrooms only, where children are most likely present
   D. Any location within 6 feet of a water source

6. A single-gang metal box contains two 12 AWG cables (4 conductors plus 2 grounds) and one device. Using the NEC box fill calculation, which box is correctly sized?
   A. A 14 cubic-inch box — it easily fits two cables
   B. A 16 cubic-inch box — just enough for the conductors
   C. An 18 cubic-inch box or larger — meeting the required fill volume for the conductors and device
   D. Box fill is not required for metal boxes under 20 cubic inches

7. When is an AFCI breaker required on a bedroom circuit in a new residential installation?
   A. Only if the circuit is longer than 50 feet
   B. In all cases — AFCI protection is required by the NEC for all bedroom branch circuits in dwellings
   C. Only when aluminum wiring is present
   D. Only for circuits supplying receptacles, not lighting circuits

8. The minimum working clearance in front of a residential panelboard is:
   A. 18 inches
   B. 24 inches
   C. 30 inches
   D. 36 inches

9. Fluorescent or LED luminaires installed in a clothes closet must maintain a minimum clearance of how many inches from the nearest point of storage space?
   A. 6 inches
   B. 12 inches
   C. 6 inches for surface-mounted, 12 inches for pendant-hung
   D. Enclosed, surface-mounted LED luminaires have no minimum clearance requirement from storage

10. An electrician is asked to bond a metal water supply pipe entering the building. The bonding conductor is connected to:
    A. The main bonding jumper inside the service panel's grounded bus
    B. Any grounded receptacle within 10 feet of the pipe
    C. The equipment ground wire of the nearest circuit
    D. The neutral conductor at the first outlet box

11. A homeowner's GFCI outlet keeps tripping when a bathroom exhaust fan runs. The most likely cause is:
    A. The circuit is overloaded with too many receptacles
    B. Moisture from the fan duct is causing a ground fault in the fan or its wiring
    C. The GFCI is protecting a downstream circuit and the fan is on a separate breaker
    D. The GFCI device itself is wired in reverse (load and line swapped)

12. Which type of cable is permitted for exposed wiring in an unfinished basement in a single-family dwelling under the NEC?
    A. NM-B (Romex) stapled to the joists — this is the standard installation
    B. NM-B installed in conduit or protected from physical damage
    C. Only MC cable or conduit wiring methods in exposed locations
    D. Any cable type, because basements are considered dry locations

13. Smoke alarms in a new residential construction must be:
    A. Battery-operated and placed only in bedrooms
    B. AC-powered with battery backup and interconnected so all alarms sound when one detects smoke
    C. Dual-sensor (ionization and photoelectric) per the NEC
    D. Hardwired to a dedicated 20-ampere circuit

14. What is the correct action when a circuit breaker trips repeatedly under normal load conditions?
    A. Replace the breaker with the next larger ampere rating
    B. Investigate for overloading, a short circuit, or a ground fault before resetting
    C. Reset the breaker twice — if it trips again, wrap it with electrical tape
    D. Install a time-delay fuse in line with the breaker

15. When installing a new 240-volt dryer circuit, the minimum conductor size for a 30-ampere circuit using copper is:
    A. 14 AWG
    B. 12 AWG
    C. 10 AWG
    D. 8 AWG

16. Where must carbon monoxide (CO) alarms be installed in a dwelling with a fuel-burning appliance?
    A. Only in the utility room containing the appliance
    B. Outside each sleeping area and on each habitable level of the dwelling
    C. At the highest point on each ceiling, since CO rises
    D. Within 6 inches of the floor, since CO is heavier than air

17. A pigtail is used at a device location when:
    A. The wire gauge changes from 12 AWG to 14 AWG at a splice
    B. Multiple cables enter the box and the device should not serve as the splice point for through-wiring
    C. The circuit must be extended to an additional outlet
    D. A ground wire needs to be connected to a metal box

18. Which of the following correctly describes the difference between the grounding and grounded conductors?
    A. They are the same conductor — the terms are interchangeable
    B. The grounded conductor (neutral) carries return current; the grounding conductor (equipment ground) carries fault current only
    C. The grounding conductor carries return current in a 240-volt circuit; neutral is unused
    D. The grounded conductor is always bare copper; the grounding conductor is white

19. An outdoor receptacle on the exterior of a house must be installed in an enclosure rated for:
    A. NEMA 1 (general purpose indoor)
    B. NEMA 3R (rainproof) minimum, or in-use cover when receptacle may be in use
    C. NEMA 4X (watertight) for all exterior locations
    D. Any standard box with a metal weatherproof plate

20. A journeyman is rough-wiring a new bathroom and must decide where to position the GFCI device. Which placement provides the broadest protection?
    A. At the last outlet in the run, to protect only that device
    B. At the panel using a GFCI breaker, protecting the entire bathroom circuit
    C. At the first outlet in the run, with downstream receptacles connected to the LOAD terminals
    D. One GFCI per receptacle — daisy-chaining GFCI protection is prohibited
