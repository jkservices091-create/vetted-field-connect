# Cabinetry & Countertops — Entrance Quiz

**Questions:** 20  |  **Passing score:** 16/20 (80%)  |  **Time limit:** 30 minutes

Instructions: Read each question carefully. Choose the single best answer. No outside resources, no phones.

---

1. When carrying full-height upper cabinets, a worker should:
   A. Carry them solo with the cabinet back facing the body for balance
   B. Use a two-person lift and keep the load close to the body, bending at the knees
   C. Slide them across the subfloor to avoid lifting injuries
   D. Stack multiple cabinets and carry them together to reduce trips

2. The first cabinet installed in a run of base cabinets should be:
   A. The cabinet nearest the centerline of the room
   B. The highest point on the floor, because all other cabinets will be shimmed up to that level
   C. Any corner cabinet, because corners establish the reference for the entire run
   D. The cabinet at the lowest point of the floor so shims are not needed behind it

3. To find the highest point on a floor before setting base cabinets, an installer should use:
   A. A 4-foot level laid flat across the floor and checked in multiple directions
   B. A tape measure from ceiling to floor at each cabinet location
   C. A laser level or long straightedge to survey the floor plane across the full run
   D. A visual inspection — high spots are typically visible as humps in the flooring

4. A scribe strip (filler) on the end of a cabinet run against a wall is used to:
   A. Add decorative trim at the exposed end panel
   B. Fill the gap between the cabinet and an out-of-plumb or irregular wall without shimming the cabinet out of alignment
   C. Provide an attachment point for the countertop overhang clip
   D. Hide the seam between two adjacent cabinet boxes

5. Wall studs are the preferred fastening point for hanging upper cabinets. If a stud is not available at the required location, an acceptable alternative is:
   A. Fastening through drywall with standard drywall screws rated for the cabinet weight
   B. Installing a horizontal wooden ledger or blocking in the wall cavity, then fastening to the ledger
   C. Using construction adhesive on the cabinet back and driving anchors into the drywall core
   D. Skipping that fastener location — two stud fasteners per cabinet are never required

6. Upper cabinets in a standard kitchen are typically hung with their bottom edge at what height above the finished floor?
   A. 48 inches
   B. 54 inches
   C. 54 to 60 inches (commonly 54 inches in lower-ceiling kitchens, 60 inches when specified)
   D. 84 inches — flush with the top of a standard 96-inch ceiling

7. When installing frameless (European-style) base cabinets side by side, the boxes are fastened together through:
   A. The toe kick only, using screws driven from below
   B. The face frame stiles, clamped and screwed together
   C. The cabinet sides (inside the cabinet), using clamps and cabinet screws placed near the front and back
   D. The back panels only, to avoid visible fasteners inside the box

8. A soft-close hinge that causes a door to hang higher on one side than the other is typically corrected by:
   A. Replacing the hinge cup with a larger diameter
   B. Adjusting the vertical adjustment screw on the mounting plate
   C. Loosening the door screws and shimming behind the hinge plate
   D. Planing the door edge to compensate

9. The standard countertop overhang beyond the face of base cabinets on a finished side (not a breakfast bar) is:
   A. 0 inches — countertops are flush with the cabinet face
   B. 1 to 1-1/2 inches
   C. 3 inches
   D. 6 inches

10. A laminate countertop seam should be located:
    A. Centered on the sink cutout for symmetry
    B. Directly over a base cabinet divider, away from the sink and high-use areas
    C. At the nearest inside corner to minimize the visible length of the seam
    D. At the midpoint of the longest countertop run

11. When making a sink cutout in a laminate countertop, the installer should:
    A. Cut from the top surface down with a circular saw at high speed for a clean edge
    B. Score the laminate with a utility knife first, then cut from below with a jigsaw to minimize chipping on the visible face
    C. Use a router with a flush-trim bit following a template for all cutout types
    D. Mark and cut with an oscillating tool, working from the top surface

12. To prevent a quartz or stone countertop from cracking at a sink cutout over time, the installer should:
    A. Caulk the underside of the stone to the cabinet top rail before setting it
    B. Ensure the countertop has continuous substrate support and apply silicone at the sink rim rather than rigid adhesive
    C. Install extra cabinet screws through the top rail into the stone
    D. Use construction adhesive at all four corners of the sink cutout

13. A dishwasher must be secured to prevent tipping forward when the door is open and the racks are loaded. The standard fastening method is:
    A. Silicone adhesive between the dishwasher sides and adjacent cabinet walls
    B. Mounting brackets fastened to the underside of the countertop or to the adjacent cabinet sides per manufacturer instructions
    C. A restraint strap around the drain hose clipped to the back of the cabinet
    D. Self-tapping screws through the dishwasher door frame into the adjacent cabinet face frame

14. A base cabinet that rocks diagonally (front-left to back-right) is corrected by:
    A. Adding a shim under the front-left or back-right leveling leg until all four corners are stable
    B. Blocking only the two rocking corners — the opposite two do not need adjustment
    C. Screwing the cabinet to the wall tight to pull it into position
    D. Adding a countertop to add weight and stabilize the cabinet

15. Silicone caulk is applied at the joint between a countertop and backsplash tile rather than grout because:
    A. Silicone bonds better to ceramic than grout does
    B. The countertop and wall move independently; a flexible sealant absorbs movement without cracking
    C. Grout requires a minimum joint width of 1/2 inch that backsplash joints rarely provide
    D. Silicone is more resistant to food staining than sanded grout

16. When installing upper cabinets without a helper, a temporary ledger board is fastened to the wall at:
    A. The height of the cabinet tops, so cabinets rest upside down during installation
    B. The height of the cabinet bottoms, so cabinets rest on the ledger at the correct elevation while being fastened
    C. Stud height at 16 inches on center so the ledger serves as a nailing strip
    D. Approximately 48 inches above the floor — the standard countertop height

17. Cabinet filler strips are ripped from solid wood or pre-finished panels and installed to:
    A. Cover the gap between the end of a cabinet run and a wall or appliance opening
    B. Add depth to shallow cabinets that don't reach a 24-inch standard depth
    C. Provide backing for mounting the countertop edge profile
    D. Replace damaged face-frame stiles on existing cabinets

18. A granite countertop slab has a hairline crack running from one corner. Before installation, the correct action is to:
    A. Install it immediately — all natural stone has veining that resembles cracks
    B. Reject the slab or document the crack and consult the homeowner before proceeding
    C. Apply polyester filler to the crack from the underside to reinforce it
    D. Route a shallow groove over the crack and fill with color-matched epoxy without disclosing it

19. The recommended fastener for securing a cabinet box to a wood stud through 1/2-inch drywall is:
    A. A 1-1/4-inch coarse-thread drywall screw
    B. A 2-1/2- to 3-inch cabinet or wood screw with a washer head, driven at the hanging rail
    C. A 3/8-inch lag bolt with a lead anchor in the drywall
    D. A finish nail driven at a 45-degree angle through the cabinet back

20. During installation, a worker notices the base cabinet run will end 3/4 inch short of the refrigerator opening width specified on the kitchen plan. The best course of action is:
    A. Force the last cabinet to close the gap using bar clamps
    B. Stop work, notify the supervisor or project manager, and document the discrepancy before proceeding
    C. Add a 3/4-inch plywood shim behind all cabinets and shift the run over
    D. Install the run as-is — appliance manufacturers allow up to 2 inches of variation
