# Landscaping & Hardscaping — Entrance Quiz

**Questions:** 20  |  **Passing score:** 16/20 (80%)  |  **Time limit:** 30 minutes

Instructions: Read each question carefully. Choose the single best answer. No outside resources, no phones.

---

1. Before any excavation begins on a job site, a worker must:
   A. Verify the dig depth with the project manager and begin immediately
   B. Contact the local utility locate service (811 in the U.S.) and wait for all utilities to be marked
   C. Probe the soil with a metal rod to locate underground utilities manually
   D. Review the property survey and proceed if no utilities are shown on the plat

2. A crew member is operating a plate compactor for extended periods. Which PPE is most critical beyond safety footwear?
   A. Hard hat
   B. High-visibility vest
   C. Hearing protection and anti-vibration gloves
   D. Chemical-resistant gloves

3. When building a segmental retaining wall, the base course should be:
   A. Set on undisturbed soil at finish grade so it stays visible for inspection
   B. Buried at least one course deep below finish grade on a compacted gravel base
   C. Set on the existing sod layer to reduce the need for excavation
   D. Placed on a 4-inch concrete footing for all walls regardless of height

4. Gravel base depth and compaction requirements for a residential patio paver installation on stable, well-draining soil typically call for:
   A. 2 inches of gravel base, uncompacted
   B. 4 inches of compacted aggregate base for pedestrian areas, 6 inches for vehicular
   C. 6 to 8 inches of compacted aggregate for all residential paver work
   D. No base — setting sand alone is sufficient if pavers are heavy enough

5. Geogrid reinforcement in a segmental block retaining wall is required when:
   A. The wall height exceeds 2 feet above finish grade
   B. The wall height exceeds the manufacturer's recommended unreinforced height (commonly 3–4 feet)
   C. The backfill soil is sandy or granular
   D. The wall is visible from the street

6. Proper drainage behind a retaining wall prevents wall failure by:
   A. Reducing the weight of the retained soil through saturation
   B. Relieving hydrostatic pressure that would otherwise push the wall outward
   C. Allowing water to flow under the wall footing during heavy rain
   D. Keeping the retained soil dry so it stays lighter than saturated soil

7. The batter (backward lean) built into a segmental retaining wall is typically:
   A. Plumb (0°) — batter is only for decorative purposes
   B. 1 inch setback per course, leaning into the slope, for structural stability
   C. 5 to 10 degrees forward (toward the viewer) for visual effect
   D. Variable — batter is only required on walls over 6 feet tall

8. A new landscape installation must direct surface drainage away from the building foundation at a minimum slope of:
   A. 1 inch over 10 feet
   B. 6 inches over the first 10 feet away from the foundation
   C. 2% slope anywhere on the property
   D. 1/4 inch per foot for the entire yard

9. An irrigation system's backflow preventer is installed to:
   A. Regulate water pressure to the irrigation heads
   B. Prevent irrigation water (which may contain fertilizer or pesticides) from siphoning back into the potable water supply
   C. Drain the irrigation lines automatically at the end of each cycle
   D. Allow the system to operate at pressures above the municipal supply pressure

10. When laying sod, the soil preparation step that most directly affects long-term success is:
    A. Raking the soil perfectly smooth before placing sod rolls
    B. Tilling to a depth of 4–6 inches and amending with topsoil or compost to establish a healthy root zone
    C. Applying a pre-emergent herbicide 24 hours before installation
    D. Wetting the subgrade heavily the day before so sod roots make immediate contact with moisture

11. Mulch applied around trees and shrubs should be:
    A. Piled high against the trunk (volcano mulch) to retain moisture at the base
    B. Applied 2–4 inches deep in a ring, kept several inches away from the trunk or stem
    C. Mixed into the top 2 inches of soil to improve drainage
    D. Applied at 6 inches or deeper to fully suppress weed growth

12. Newly planted trees 1.5 inches in caliper or larger are staked primarily to:
    A. Permanently support the trunk since roots cannot anchor the tree on their own
    B. Protect the trunk from mower and foot traffic damage on the job site
    C. Stabilize the root ball during establishment — stakes must be removed after one growing season
    D. Mark the tree location for the irrigation installer

13. What is the recommended minimum planting distance from a building foundation for large shrubs (6+ feet mature height)?
    A. 1 foot — foundations provide good reflected heat for growth
    B. 3 feet minimum — matching the plant's mature spread to the wall
    C. At least half the plant's mature spread, kept away from foundation drains and walls
    D. 10 feet for all shrubs to prevent root intrusion into the foundation

14. When cutting segmental pavers with a dry-cut diamond blade, a worker must:
    A. Work indoors to control dust spread to neighboring properties
    B. Use respiratory protection (N95 minimum) and wet-cut when possible to control silica dust
    C. Wear only safety glasses — the dust from concrete pavers is non-toxic
    D. Run the saw at low RPM to reduce heat without respiratory protection

15. A French drain is installed to:
    A. Redirect surface water from a downspout away from the foundation
    B. Collect and convey subsurface groundwater away from an area through a perforated pipe in a gravel trench
    C. Provide an irrigation supply line below the frost line
    D. Drain water from a retaining wall weep hole directly into the municipal sewer

16. Plant hardiness zones are used primarily to determine:
    A. The amount of water a plant needs in a given climate
    B. Whether a plant can survive the minimum winter temperatures in a geographic region
    C. The best season to apply fertilizer for each species
    D. The maximum heat tolerance of a plant in summer months

17. When compacting layers of aggregate base for a paver installation, each lift should not exceed:
    A. 2 inches per lift
    B. 4 inches per lift before compacting
    C. 6 inches per lift — a plate compactor compacts to this depth in one pass
    D. 8 inches per lift if a jumping jack compactor is used

18. Setting sand under concrete pavers should be screeded to a depth of:
    A. 1/2 inch
    B. 1 inch
    C. 2 inches
    D. 3 inches

19. After installing pavers, joint sand is swept into the joints and then:
    A. Wetted lightly with a hose — water consolidates the sand and the job is complete
    B. Compacted with the plate compactor (with a pad protector) and sand re-swept until joints are full
    C. Sealed immediately with a polymeric sealer to lock the sand in place
    D. Left to settle naturally over the first rain cycle before any additional compaction

20. A homeowner asks a crew to remove a tree with a trunk diameter greater than 12 inches that is within 10 feet of the house. The correct response is:
    A. Proceed — experienced landscapers can safely fell trees of any size near structures
    B. Use a chainsaw with a wedge cut and directional fall away from the house
    C. Decline and refer to a licensed/certified arborist or tree service, as this work exceeds general landscaping scope and risk tolerance
    D. Remove the tree only if the homeowner signs a liability waiver
