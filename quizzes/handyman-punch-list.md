# Handyman & Punch List — Entrance Quiz

**Questions:** 20  |  **Passing score:** 16/20 (80%)  |  **Time limit:** 30 minutes

Instructions: Read each question carefully. Choose the single best answer. No outside resources, no phones.

---

1. Before drilling into a wall to mount a heavy mirror, a handyman should:
   A. Tap the wall with a knuckle — a solid sound confirms a stud
   B. Use a stud finder and verify with a small finish nail before committing to the final hole location
   C. Measure 16 inches from the nearest corner, which always locates a stud
   D. Drill a small exploratory hole at the center of the planned location

2. A homeowner reports a slow drip from the base of a toilet. The most likely cause is:
   A. A failed wax ring seal between the toilet horn and the closet flange
   B. A cracked water supply line at the shut-off valve
   C. A failed flapper allowing tank water to run into the bowl constantly
   D. High water pressure causing the fill valve to overflow into the bowl

3. Silicone caulk is the correct choice (over latex caulk) when sealing:
   A. Gaps between painted wood trim and drywall in a bedroom
   B. The joint between a bathtub surround and the tub deck in a wet area
   C. A nail hole in a painted interior door casing
   D. The seam between two sections of pre-primed baseboard

4. When patching a 4-inch diameter hole in drywall, the most reliable backing method is:
   A. Stuffing the hole with newspaper and applying joint compound in layers
   B. Using a self-adhesive mesh patch alone for holes up to 6 inches
   C. Cutting the hole square, installing wood or metal backing clips or a wood backer, then installing a cut patch
   D. Applying a drywall patch kit with a peel-and-stick aluminum face plate

5. A hollow-wall anchor (toggle bolt or expansion anchor) is selected over a wood-screw-into-stud when:
   A. The item to be mounted weighs more than 20 pounds
   B. The mounting location falls between studs and the load requires firm anchorage in drywall
   C. The wall is a load-bearing wall and studs must not be drilled
   D. The finish is painted drywall, which does not hold screws well

6. A door is sticking at the top corner on the latch side. Before planing the door, the handyman should first:
   A. Remove the door and plane the full latch edge
   B. Check that all hinge screws are tight — a loose hinge causes the door to sag and stick at the top latch corner
   C. Apply paste wax to the door edges to reduce friction
   D. Add a third hinge to the door to distribute the weight

7. A battery-operated smoke alarm chirps once every 30 to 60 seconds. This indicates:
   A. The alarm has detected a low level of smoke and should be investigated
   B. The battery is low and must be replaced
   C. The alarm has reached end-of-life and is signaling for replacement
   D. The detector head is dusty and needs cleaning

8. Smoke alarms should be installed in which location relative to the ceiling?
   A. On the wall, 12 inches below the ceiling, in every room
   B. On the ceiling or on the wall between 4 and 12 inches below the ceiling
   C. On the ceiling only — wall mounting is not permitted
   D. At least 36 inches from any corner, centered on the ceiling

9. A handyman is replacing a light fixture and notes the ceiling electrical box moves when pulled. Before hanging the new fixture, the correct action is:
   A. Wrap the box with duct tape to stiffen it, then install the fixture
   B. Replace the box with a listed fan-rated or fixture-rated box properly secured to framing or with a brace bar
   C. Install the fixture using longer screws to reach the stud behind the box
   D. Hang lighter fixtures only — the existing box is adequate for fixtures under 15 pounds

10. When touching up painted walls with a brush to cover small repairs, the paint appears to match in the can but looks different when dry. The most likely reason is:
    A. The touch-up paint was mixed incorrectly at the store
    B. The existing wall paint has aged and faded, causing a sheen or color mismatch with fresh paint
    C. Latex paint always appears darker when dry than when wet
    D. The repair compound was not primed, causing the touch-up coat to absorb differently

11. Polyurethane caulk is best suited for which application compared to silicone or latex?
    A. Around a bathtub in a tile wet area
    B. Sealing exterior gaps between wood siding and window trim where paintability and durability are both required
    C. Interior painted trim in rooms with low humidity
    D. Concrete cracks in a basement floor

12. A handyman is asked to reset a GFCI outlet that will not reset and has no power on the load side. The correct diagnostic sequence is:
    A. Replace the GFCI immediately — devices that won't reset are always failed
    B. Check that the outlet is not the downstream load of another tripped GFCI upstream, then check the breaker, then test and replace the device if faulty
    C. Reset the main breaker panel and try again
    D. Swap the line and load wires and attempt to reset

13. When grouting wall tile, grout should NOT be used to fill:
    A. The joints between adjacent wall tiles
    B. The joint at the inside corner between two tiled walls
    C. A large crack in the substrate behind the tile
    D. The joint between tiles that are different thicknesses

14. A running toilet (water constantly flowing into the bowl without flushing) is most commonly caused by:
    A. A faulty pressure regulator on the supply line
    B. A worn or misaligned flapper that does not fully seal the flush valve
    C. A cracked toilet tank allowing air to enter the fill system
    D. An oversized water supply line delivering too much pressure

15. A handyman is completing a punch list and finds a doorbell that does not work. After checking that the transformer has power, the next step is:
    A. Replace the chime unit — it is the most common failure point
    B. Press the button while an assistant listens at the chime — if no sound, test button continuity with a multimeter
    C. Replace the push button first since it is the easiest component to swap
    D. Check the wire gauge — undersized wire is the most common doorbell fault

16. To assess whether a wall outlet circuit can handle an additional load, the handyman should:
    A. Estimate the load based on the appliance wattage and verify the breaker size is not already near its rated amperage
    B. Plug in the new appliance and watch for 10 minutes — if the breaker doesn't trip, it is safe
    C. Check that the outlet is a 20-ampere receptacle (T-slot) before adding any load
    D. Consult the homeowner — only they know what else is on the circuit

17. A task requires mounting a 55-inch TV (approximately 45 lbs with mount) to a wall. The correct mounting method is:
    A. Use heavy-duty drywall anchors rated for 50 lbs each at four points
    B. Locate and fasten through at least two wall studs using lag bolts or structural screws appropriate for the mount's rating
    C. Use a French cleat system attached only to the drywall surface
    D. Any mount rated for 45 lbs is acceptable regardless of fastener type

18. A work task involves applying a bead of caulk along a long joint. The correct technique for a clean bead is:
    A. Push the gun away from you, letting the caulk pile up ahead of the nozzle
    B. Pull the gun toward you at a consistent angle and speed, tooling the bead immediately with a wet finger or caulk tool
    C. Apply a thick bead and allow it to slump into the joint before tooling
    D. Cut the nozzle at 90 degrees for the widest possible bead on all joints

19. A handyman discovers active knob-and-tube wiring while replacing an outlet in an older home. The correct action is:
    A. Connect the new outlet to the existing knob-and-tube wiring using wire nuts and proceed
    B. Stop work on the electrical portion, inform the homeowner, and recommend evaluation and replacement by a licensed electrician
    C. Install a GFCI outlet to make the ungrounded knob-and-tube circuit safe for continued use
    D. Cap the knob-and-tube wires and run a new NM-B cable from the nearest outlet

20. A homeowner asks a handyman to add a new 240-volt circuit for an EV charger in the garage. The correct response is:
    A. Pull the permit and run the circuit — 240-volt wiring is within handyman scope
    B. Decline and refer to a licensed electrician, as new panel circuit work typically exceeds handyman licensing scope and permit requirements
    C. Install the outlet and advise the homeowner to have it inspected afterward
    D. Add the circuit only if the main panel has an open breaker slot available
