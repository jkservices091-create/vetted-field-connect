# Framing & Rough Carpentry — Entrance Quiz

**Questions:** 20  |  **Passing score:** 16/20 (80%)  |  **Time limit:** 30 minutes

Instructions: Read each question carefully. Choose the single best answer. No outside resources, no phones.

---

1. When selecting framing lumber, a stud with a crown (bow) should be installed:
   A. Crown down, so gravity flattens it over time
   B. Crown toward the interior, so drywall hides it
   C. Crown up in floor joists and crown out (toward the exterior) in wall studs
   D. Discarded; crowned lumber must never be used in structural framing

2. What is the standard on-center spacing for bearing wall studs in most residential construction?
   A. 12 inches
   B. 16 inches
   C. 24 inches
   D. 19.2 inches

3. Fire blocking in stud walls is required at which location?
   A. Only at the top plate level
   B. At every floor level and at mid-height of walls exceeding 10 feet, per IRC
   C. Only in walls adjacent to attached garages
   D. At every 8 feet regardless of wall height

4. Which member carries the load over a window or door opening and transfers it to the trimmer studs?
   A. Cripple stud
   B. King stud
   C. Header
   D. Rim joist

5. What is the purpose of a trimmer (jack) stud in a door or window rough opening?
   A. To carry the load from the header down to the bottom plate or floor
   B. To provide nailing for the interior window casing
   C. To block the gap between the header and the top plate
   D. To transfer lateral wind loads to the sheathing

6. Wall sheathing panels applied in a horizontal orientation must be:
   A. Glued and nailed at 6-inch centers on all edges
   B. Blocked at all horizontal joints so every panel edge bears on solid framing or blocking
   C. Oriented so the strong axis runs parallel to the studs
   D. Limited to 3/8-inch thickness when horizontal

7. Hurricane ties or rafter ties are required at every rafter-to-top-plate connection primarily to resist:
   A. Downward gravity loads on the rafters
   B. Lateral sliding of the wall under seismic loads
   C. Uplift and racking forces from wind
   D. Shrinkage of the top plate over time

8. When squaring a wall before standing it, a carpenter checks that:
   A. The diagonal measurements from corner to corner are equal
   B. The stud spacing is exactly 16 inches on center at every bay
   C. The top and bottom plates are perfectly straight before layout
   D. The anchor bolts align with the center of the bottom plate

9. What is the minimum required nailing for structural wall sheathing edges in a standard shear wall application?
   A. 8d nails at 12 inches on center at edges
   B. 8d nails at 6 inches on center at edges and 12 inches in the field
   C. 10d nails at 4 inches on center at edges only
   D. Varies by shear wall schedule; the structural drawings govern

10. A rim joist (band joist) in platform framing serves primarily to:
    A. Carry point loads from posts above directly to the foundation
    B. Close off the open ends of floor joists and provide a nailing surface for sheathing
    C. Act as a ledger for the deck framing
    D. Provide lateral bracing between floor joists at mid-span

11. Header sizing in a bearing wall is primarily determined by:
    A. The thickness of the wall only
    B. The span of the opening and the load carried above it
    C. The height of the rough opening
    D. The species of lumber being used

12. What is the correct nailing pattern when face-nailing two 2x10s together to form a built-up header?
    A. 16d nails at 16 inches on center in two rows, staggered
    B. 12d nails at 24 inches on center in one row
    C. 10d nails at 6 inches on center along one edge only
    D. Construction adhesive only; nails are not required for built-up headers

13. Which of the following describes the correct installation of floor sheathing (subfloor) panels?
    A. Panels laid with all end joints continuous (in-line) across the floor
    B. Panels staggered so end joints are offset by at least one joist bay, with the long dimension perpendicular to the joists
    C. Panels installed with the long dimension parallel to the joists for maximum stiffness
    D. Panels gapped 1/8 inch at all edges and 1/4 inch at all ends

14. What is the standard rough opening width for a 3-0 (36-inch) exterior door?
    A. 36 inches
    B. 37-1/2 inches
    C. 38 inches
    D. 40 inches

15. When doubling the top plate of a bearing wall, the lap splice of the upper plate must:
    A. Occur directly over a stud
    B. Occur at a corner or T-intersection, offset from the lower plate splice by at least 48 inches
    C. Align with the lower plate splice at each corner
    D. Be located at mid-span between studs to avoid stress concentration

16. Which PPE is specifically required when operating a pneumatic nail gun overhead?
    A. Hard hat and steel-toed boots only
    B. Safety glasses or goggles and hearing protection
    C. Cut-resistant gloves and a face shield
    D. A dust respirator and safety glasses

17. At what height above a lower level does OSHA require fall protection for residential construction workers?
    A. 4 feet
    B. 6 feet
    C. 8 feet
    D. 10 feet

18. A carpenter lays out a 16-inch on-center stud wall and marks the first stud at 15-1/4 inches from the corner. Why?
    A. To account for the corner assembly thickness so the sheathing panel breaks on stud centers
    B. To leave room for the anchor bolt at the corner
    C. It is an error; the first stud should always be at 16 inches
    D. To maintain 16-inch spacing after adding the king stud at the opening

19. Solid blocking installed between floor joists directly under a bearing wall above serves to:
    A. Increase the span capacity of the joists
    B. Transfer the wall load into the joists and prevent joist rotation
    C. Provide a nailing base for ceiling drywall
    D. Act as the required fire block between floors

20. When framing a non-load-bearing interior partition wall that runs parallel to floor joists, how must it be supported?
    A. It can be placed anywhere on the subfloor without additional support
    B. It must sit directly over a floor joist or over solid blocking installed between joists
    C. It requires a doubled joist beneath it regardless of wall weight
    D. It is hung from the ceiling joists with metal hangers
