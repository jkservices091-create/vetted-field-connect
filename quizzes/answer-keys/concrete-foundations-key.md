# Concrete Foundations — Answer Key

1. **B** — IRC Section R403.1.4 requires footings to be located below the frost depth to prevent heave from freeze-thaw cycles. The local jurisdiction's frost line map or table determines this minimum depth.

2. **D** — ACI 318 and the IRC require a minimum of 3 inches of concrete cover over reinforcing steel in footings cast against and permanently exposed to earth. This protects the steel from corrosion.

3. **C** — Adding water or other site modifications to a delivered mix voids the design and batch plant certification. The load should be rejected or the batch plant contacted to determine whether the design is within tolerance before placement.

4. **B** — A vapor barrier (Class I vapor retarder per IRC R506.2.3) prevents soil moisture from wicking up through the slab, which can damage floor coverings, cause efflorescence, and contribute to mold growth.

5. **B** — Anchor bolts must be set in the wet concrete at the correct spacing and embedment depth per the structural drawings. Post-installed epoxy anchors are an alternative but require engineer approval and specific installation procedures.

6. **B** — ACI 306 cold-weather concreting procedures apply when the air temperature is below 40°F or when temperatures are forecast to fall below 40°F within 24 hours of placement. Concrete curing can be severely compromised or halted below this threshold.

7. **B** — Form-release agents (oils, waxes, or chemical compounds) coat the form surface to prevent adhesion between the concrete and the form, allowing clean stripping without damaging the form or the concrete face.

8. **C** — Lap splices must be staggered and placed in low-stress zones per ACI 318 requirements. Clustering splices at high-stress or high-moment locations concentrates weakness and can lead to crack propagation.

9. **B** — Damp-proofing (a thin asphalt or bituminous coating) is the code minimum for below-grade walls in many conditions; full waterproofing membranes are required where hydrostatic pressure exists. Paint is not a code-compliant below-grade treatment.

10. **B** — A construction joint in concrete must be cleaned of laitance, dirt, and loose material. A bonding agent or neat cement slurry scrubbed into the surface before the fresh pour improves bond and reduces the risk of a cold joint plane becoming a weakness.

11. **D** — For tension lap splices in structural concrete, ACI 318 and most residential structural specifications require 40–48 bar diameters, which for #4 bar (½-inch diameter) equals 20–24 inches. Always defer to the engineer's specification; IRC tables provide minimums that may be exceeded by plans.

12. **B** — High ambient temperatures accelerate hydration, causing rapid evaporation of mix water, premature stiffening (false set), plastic shrinkage cracking, and reduced long-term strength if proper precautions (chilled water, ice, sun shading, retarders) are not used.

13. **C** — Most standard specifications and manufacturer guidance require at least 7 days of cure before backfilling to allow the wall to develop sufficient strength to resist lateral earth pressure without formwork support.

14. **C** — IRC R403.1.6 requires anchor bolts to be embedded a minimum of 7 inches into concrete foundation walls. Insufficient embedment can allow the sill plate to pull off under lateral loads such as wind or seismic forces.

15. **B** — IRC R403.1.5 requires the horizontal distance between steps in a stepped footing to be at least 2 feet (with the step height not exceeding the footing thickness). This ensures stable bearing and prevents undermining of the upper step.

16. **C** — Cutting and grinding hardened concrete generates respirable crystalline silica dust, which causes silicosis. OSHA's Silica Standard (29 CFR 1926.1153) requires respiratory protection; eye and hearing protection address secondary hazards from dust and noise.

17. **B** — The slump test (ASTM C143) measures the consistency and workability of fresh concrete by recording the vertical drop of a concrete cone after the mold is removed. It does not directly measure strength or air content.

18. **B** — Isolation joints (also called expansion joints) completely separate the slab from fixed elements like columns, walls, or drains, allowing independent vertical and horizontal movement and preventing cracking caused by differential settlement or restraint.

19. **B** — The correct sequence is compacted subgrade first (providing stable, uniform support), then a gravel base for drainage and load distribution, then the vapor barrier to block moisture, then reinforcement positioned above the vapor barrier, and finally the concrete pour.

20. **C** — Concrete pumps operate at high pressure, and a blocked hose stores significant energy. The pump must be shut down and pressure completely relieved before attempting to clear the blockage. Striking a pressurized hose or increasing pressure can cause hose burst or sudden movement that injures nearby workers.
