# Windows & Doors — Entrance Quiz

**Questions:** 20  |  **Passing score:** 16/20 (80%)  |  **Time limit:** 30 minutes

Instructions: Read each question carefully. Choose the single best answer. No outside resources, no phones.

---

1. When installing a window unit, the installer checks plumb, level, and square. Which check confirms the window frame is not racked (twisted)?
   A. Checking level on the sill only
   B. Checking that both diagonal measurements of the frame are equal
   C. Checking plumb on both jambs independently
   D. Checking that the unit is centered in the rough opening

2. What is the standard rough opening (RO) size for a window with a listed unit size of 3-0 x 4-0 (36 inches wide x 48 inches tall)?
   A. Exactly 36 x 48 inches
   B. 37-1/2 x 49-1/2 inches
   C. 38 x 50 inches
   D. 36-1/2 x 48-1/2 inches

3. A sill pan flashing under a window is intended to:
   A. Provide a level bearing surface so shims are not required at the sill
   B. Collect any water that infiltrates behind the window unit and direct it to drain to the exterior
   C. Thermally isolate the window frame from the rough sill
   D. Prevent air infiltration under the window sill

4. When applying self-adhered flashing tape around a window rough opening, the correct sequence is:
   A. Head first, then sill, then jambs
   B. Sill first, then jambs, then head — so each successive piece laps over the one below it
   C. Jambs first, then sill, then head
   D. Head first, then jambs, then sill

5. Where should sealant (caulk) be applied on the exterior of a newly installed window?
   A. Along the sill only, to seal the gap between the window frame and the rough sill
   B. On the sides (jambs) and head, but NOT under the sill — the sill must remain open to drain
   C. Around the full perimeter of the window, including under the sill
   D. Only at the trim-to-siding joint; no caulk should touch the window frame itself

6. When shimming a window unit, shims should be placed:
   A. Only at the corners of the rough opening
   B. At the hinge and latch locations on the jambs, and under the sill corners, so the frame is uniformly supported
   C. In the center of each jamb only
   D. At random intervals wherever gaps are visible

7. Per IRC egress requirements, a sleeping room window must have a minimum net clear opening of:
   A. 3.0 square feet with a minimum opening height of 18 inches
   B. 5.7 square feet with a minimum opening height of 24 inches
   C. 5.7 square feet with a minimum opening height of 24 inches and a maximum sill height of 44 inches above the finished floor
   D. 3.6 square feet with no minimum dimension requirement

8. Which egress requirement applies to the maximum height of a window sill above the finished floor in a sleeping room?
   A. 36 inches
   B. 44 inches
   C. 48 inches
   D. 54 inches

9. Exterior door thresholds must be adjusted so that:
   A. The door sweeps the threshold without touching it when closed
   B. The door compresses the weatherstrip seal at the threshold when closed, with no visible daylight gap
   C. A 1/8-inch gap is maintained for drainage
   D. The threshold sits flush with the interior finish floor regardless of exterior conditions

10. When installing an exterior door in a new rough opening, the installer finds that one corner of the sill is 1/4 inch lower than the other. The correct action is to:
    A. Shim under the low corner of the door unit to bring the sill to level before fastening
    B. Plane the top of the low corner of the rough sill to bring it to level
    C. Proceed with installation and adjust the threshold plate to compensate
    D. Pack the gap with expanding foam before setting the door

11. What material is specifically recommended for filling gaps between a window or door frame and the rough opening framing for air sealing?
    A. Standard expanding spray foam at full expansion
    B. Minimal-expanding foam (door and window foam) or backer rod with caulk
    C. Fiberglass batt insulation packed tightly
    D. OSB cut to fill the gap and nailed in place

12. Flashing tape at the sill of a window rough opening must be:
    A. Applied to dry framing only; the surface must be clean, dry, and free of dust
    B. Applied over a wet housewrap surface so it bonds to the moisture barrier
    C. Heated with a torch before application in all temperatures
    D. Applied over backer rod to fill the gap before taping

13. What is the correct installation sequence for integrating a window with the housewrap (WRB) drainage plane?
    A. Install window → install housewrap → tape all four sides of the window flange
    B. Install housewrap → cut and fold sill flap into RO → flash sill → set window → flash jambs → flash head → fold head flap over head flashing → tape
    C. Install housewrap → set window → tape head → tape jambs → tape sill
    D. Set window → wrap housewrap flaps over all four flanges → tape

14. Exterior wood trim around windows and doors should be back-primed before installation primarily to:
    A. Improve adhesion of the finish coat applied after installation
    B. Seal the back face and ends against moisture absorption, reducing cupping and rot
    C. Comply with OSHA requirements for chemical handling on job sites
    D. Speed up the drying time of the exterior paint system

15. A newly installed exterior door binds at the top corner of the latch side after installation. What is the most likely cause?
    A. The hinge side jamb is not plumb
    B. The door itself is crowned and needs to be replaced
    C. The strike plate is installed too high
    D. Insufficient weatherstrip compression at the head

16. Impact-resistant (hurricane) windows are required in certain geographic zones. What test standard is commonly referenced to verify their performance?
    A. ASTM E330 structural test only
    B. AAMA/WDMA/CSA 101/I.S.2/A440 (NAFS) certification
    C. ANSI/BHMA door hardware standards
    D. ICC-ES evaluation report for the framing system only

17. Which of the following is the correct practice when nailing a window flange to the rough opening framing?
    A. Drive nails through the slots in the flange at an angle to pull the unit tight to the framing
    B. Drive nails straight through the flange slots, leaving the unit slightly loose until level and plumb are confirmed, then fasten fully
    C. Fasten the head flange first, then hang the unit from the top before checking level

18. At what height above a lower level does OSHA require fall protection for workers installing windows from the exterior?
    A. 4 feet
    B. 6 feet
    C. 8 feet
    D. 10 feet

19. A window installer is setting a large unit and notices the rough opening is 1 inch wider than specified. The best corrective action before setting the window is to:
    A. Center the window in the opening and use extra-thick shims to fill the gap
    B. Sister a 2x4 or filler strip to the trimmer stud to bring the RO to the correct width, then set the window
    C. Proceed with installation; the extra width can be covered by interior casing
    D. Reject the window unit; a non-standard RO requires a custom-sized unit

20. Weatherstripping on an exterior door is considered properly adjusted when:
    A. The door closes easily with no resistance from the weatherstrip
    B. Slight resistance is felt when closing, and no light or air passes through the perimeter when the door is closed and latched
    C. The weatherstrip is compressed halfway to its unloaded thickness when the door is latched
    D. There is a uniform 1/16-inch gap visible around the door perimeter
