# Drywall & Plaster — Entrance Quiz

**Questions:** 20  |  **Passing score:** 16/20 (80%)  |  **Time limit:** 30 minutes

Instructions: Read each question carefully. Choose the single best answer. No outside resources, no phones.

---

1. Which drywall panel thickness is most commonly used for standard residential walls with studs at 16 inches on center?
   A. ¼ inch
   B. ⅜ inch
   C. ½ inch
   D. ⅝ inch

2. According to standard drywall installation practice, screws on field (non-edge) areas of a wall panel should be spaced no more than:
   A. 8 inches on center
   B. 12 inches on center
   C. 16 inches on center
   D. 24 inches on center

3. A drywall screw is driven correctly when:
   A. The head dimples the face paper slightly without breaking through it
   B. The head is flush with the panel surface
   C. The head is driven until it is just below the back paper
   D. The head protrudes slightly to allow for compound feathering

4. In a Type X fire-rated wall assembly, what is the most common drywall panel thickness used to achieve a 1-hour rating?
   A. ½ inch standard gypsum
   B. ⅝ inch Type X gypsum
   C. ¾ inch moisture-resistant board
   D. ¼ inch flexible gypsum over standard ½ inch

5. What is the purpose of a control joint in a large drywall ceiling?
   A. To allow electrical conduit to pass through the ceiling plane
   B. To relieve stress caused by structural movement and thermal expansion, preventing cracking
   C. To provide a break point for installing different paint colors
   D. To mark the boundary between fire-rated and non-rated ceiling zones

6. Which dust hazard is most significant when sanding drywall joint compound?
   A. Lead dust from the pigments in pre-mixed compound
   B. Crystalline silica dust, which can cause silicosis with repeated exposure
   C. Asbestos fibers from the gypsum core
   D. Calcium chloride vapors released by chemical-set compounds

7. A Level 5 finish is specified for a project. Which step is unique to Level 5 that is not part of a Level 4 finish?
   A. Embedding joint tape with taping compound
   B. Applying a skim coat or topping compound skim over the entire panel face
   C. Filling all fastener depressions with compound
   D. Feathering the second coat of compound 10 inches wide over taped joints

8. When hanging drywall on a ceiling, panels should be:
   A. Installed with all seams running parallel to the framing
   B. Fastened to each framing member at the edges only
   C. Installed perpendicular to the framing to minimize the number of butt joints
   D. Installed with the long edge parallel to the framing to maximize panel span

9. Which type of corner bead is most appropriate for an outside corner on a high-traffic wall where impact resistance is the priority?
   A. Paper-faced vinyl bead
   B. Expanded metal bead
   C. Vinyl flexible bead
   D. Bullnose paper bead

10. Moisture-resistant (MR or "greenboard") drywall is appropriate in which of the following locations?
    A. As a direct tile substrate in a shower surround
    B. As a wall substrate in areas of incidental moisture, such as behind a vanity
    C. As the substrate for the shower pan liner
    D. On exterior walls as a weather-resistant barrier

11. What is the main difference between taping compound (all-purpose or setting type) and topping compound?
    A. Taping compound shrinks more and is harder; topping compound is lighter and sands more easily
    B. Topping compound contains more aggregate and is used for the base coat only
    C. Taping compound is only available in powder form; topping compound is only pre-mixed
    D. There is no functional difference; they can always be substituted for each other

12. You arrive at a job and notice the previously applied compound coat has dried with visible ridges and tool marks. What is the correct next step?
    A. Apply the next coat immediately over the ridges to build them out
    B. Knock down ridges with a broad knife and lightly sand before applying the next coat
    C. Wet the surface and re-trowel the existing coat
    D. Apply a skim of setting-type compound over the ridges and proceed

13. Which fastener is preferred for attaching drywall to metal studs?
    A. Ring-shank nails
    B. Fine-thread drywall screws
    C. Coarse-thread drywall screws
    D. Staples at 4-inch intervals

14. In a fire-rated assembly, butt joints on opposite sides of the same stud:
    A. Are required to share the same stud to ensure continuity
    B. Must be offset to different studs to maintain the assembly's fire-resistance rating
    C. Can be placed on the same stud if both are screwed at 8 inches on center
    D. Are prohibited; only tapered edge joints are allowed in fire-rated assemblies

15. What is the recommended maximum gap between the bottom of a drywall panel and a concrete slab floor?
    A. No gap is needed; the panel should touch the slab
    B. ⅛ inch
    C. ½ inch
    D. 1 inch

16. When applying base-coat plaster (brown coat) over a scratch coat, the correct approach is to:
    A. Apply the brown coat before the scratch coat has cured at all
    B. Wait until the scratch coat has been moist-cured and is firm but not fully dry, then apply
    C. Apply immediately after raking the scratch coat while it is still wet
    D. Apply only after the scratch coat has dried completely and hardened for at least 7 days

17. A journeyman notices that taped joints are photographing (becoming visible) through the paint in raking light. The most likely cause is:
    A. Using setting-type compound instead of topping compound on the final coat
    B. Feathering the compound too wide on the finish coat
    C. Insufficient coats or inadequate feathering, leaving crowned ridges at joint edges
    D. Applying primer before the compound was fully dry

18. At what interval should control joints be placed in a continuous drywall ceiling that has no perimeter relief?
    A. Every 8 feet
    B. Every 30 feet
    C. Every 50 feet
    D. Only at the centerline of the ceiling, regardless of span

19. Which personal protective equipment is required when spraying texture compound with a hopper gun?
    A. Chemical splash goggles and a supplied-air respirator
    B. Safety glasses and an N95 respirator at minimum
    C. A face shield alone is sufficient
    D. No respiratory protection is needed for water-based texture

20. A crew is drywalling a garage ceiling that is the underside of a living space above. Which product is required by most building codes for this application?
    A. ½-inch standard drywall
    B. ⅜-inch moisture-resistant drywall
    C. ⅝-inch Type X fire-rated drywall
    D. ¼-inch flexible drywall with two layers
