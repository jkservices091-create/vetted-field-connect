# Insulation & Air Sealing — Entrance Quiz

**Questions:** 20  |  **Passing score:** 16/20 (80%)  |  **Time limit:** 30 minutes

Instructions: Read each question carefully. Choose the single best answer. No outside resources, no phones.

---

1. When installing fiberglass batt insulation, you notice the cavity is 2 inches deeper than the batt thickness. What is the correct action?
   A. Fold the excess batt material back on itself to fill the gap
   B. Use the correct-depth batt or supplement with a cut strip to fill the full cavity depth
   C. Install the batt as-is; the air gap behind it acts as additional insulation
   D. Pack the batt tightly against the interior face and ignore the gap at the back

2. Which respiratory protection is required when installing loose-fill fiberglass insulation in an enclosed attic space?
   A. A dust mask (surgical-style) is sufficient
   B. No respiratory protection is needed if the attic is ventilated
   C. An N95 or higher particulate respirator
   D. A full-face supplied-air respirator is always required by OSHA

3. What does the R-value of insulation measure?
   A. The rate at which moisture vapor passes through the material
   B. The material's resistance to heat flow
   C. The compressive strength of the insulation product
   D. The air permeance rating per square foot

4. An air barrier is designed primarily to:
   A. Slow the diffusion of water vapor through building assemblies
   B. Increase the R-value of adjacent insulation layers
   C. Resist bulk airflow through the building envelope
   D. Prevent condensation on cold surfaces inside wall cavities

5. Which of the following locations requires an IC-rated recessed light fixture?
   A. A fixture installed in a vaulted ceiling with no insulation above
   B. A fixture that will be covered or in contact with attic insulation
   C. A fixture mounted on an interior wall with no exterior exposure
   D. Any fixture within 24 inches of a bathroom exhaust fan

6. You are air-sealing the top plates of interior walls before blowing attic insulation. Which material is most appropriate for large, irregular gaps around wiring and plumbing penetrations?
   A. Fiberglass batt stuffed into the gap
   B. Two-part closed-cell spray foam
   C. Kraft-faced batt stapled over the opening
   D. Housewrap tape applied over the gap from above

7. What is the primary purpose of attic baffles (rafter vents)?
   A. To add R-value between the rafter bays
   B. To maintain a clear airflow channel from the soffit vent to the ridge vent
   C. To prevent rodents from entering the attic through the soffits
   D. To act as a vapor barrier on the cold side of the insulation

8. Closed-cell spray polyurethane foam (ccSPF) differs from open-cell SPF in that it:
   A. Has a lower R-value per inch and is vapor-permeable
   B. Expands more aggressively and is best for air-sealing only
   C. Has a higher R-value per inch and acts as a vapor retarder
   D. Is always applied at greater thicknesses than open-cell foam

9. A blower door test is used to:
   A. Measure the total R-value of a wall assembly
   B. Identify and quantify air leakage in a building's envelope
   C. Test the static pressure of the HVAC duct system
   D. Verify that attic ventilation meets IRC free-area requirements

10. Under OSHA standards, when are you required to wear eye protection while installing spray foam insulation?
    A. Only when working overhead
    B. Only when using a pressurized two-component system
    C. Whenever there is a reasonable probability of eye contact with the material
    D. Eye protection is optional if a respirator with face shield is not available

11. Spray polyurethane foam applied to the underside of roof sheathing in an unvented attic assembly typically requires what additional treatment before the space is occupied?
    A. A coat of fiberglass-reinforced primer
    B. An approved ignition barrier or thermal barrier
    C. A layer of rigid mineral wool over the foam
    D. A vapor-permeable membrane stapled over the foam face

12. Which vapor retarder class is appropriate for a cold-climate wall where the designer specifies a "smart" or variable-perm membrane?
    A. Class I (≤0.1 perm) — foil or glass
    B. Class II (0.1–1.0 perm) — kraft-faced batt
    C. Class III (1–10 perm) — latex paint
    D. Class II/III variable — a membrane whose permeance changes with relative humidity

13. When installing batt insulation in a wall cavity, compression of the batt beyond the cavity depth will:
    A. Have no effect on the thermal performance
    B. Increase the effective R-value because the fibers are denser
    C. Reduce the effective R-value of the installed batt

    A. 1–1.5 lb/ft³
    B. 3.5–4 lb/ft³
