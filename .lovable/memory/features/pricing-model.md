# FieldHands pricing model — sliding scale, rating-driven

The platform makes money two ways, and BOTH sides are rewarded for being good to work with. Good ratings = lower fees. This is the core promise.

## Hirer side (pays the platform on top of the job total)
- **Free to sign up, post jobs, and review bids.** No subscriptions, no posting fees.
- When a hirer books a worker, they pay a **service fee on top of the agreed job total**.
- Fee is **sliding scale: 10% (best-rated) → 15% (default / lower-rated)**.
  - **New hirers start at 15%** — earn it down with good worker reviews.
  - 5★ average → 10%. 4★ → 12%. 3★ and below → 15%.
- Fee covers overhead, vetting, support, payments, and a small platform profit.
- Example: $200 job total, new hirer (15%) → hirer pays $230.

## Worker side (small cut taken from their wage, sliding by rating)
- **100% free to sign up, get verified, browse, and bid.** No application fees, no subscriptions.
- After a job completes, FieldHands takes a small cut from the worker's payout, **sliding by their rating: 2% (best-rated) → 10% (lowest-rated)**.
  - **New workers start at 5%** — earn it down with good ratings.
  - 5★ → 2%. 4★ → 4%. 3★ → 6%. 2★ → 8%. 1★ → 10%.
- This is how high performers are rewarded — **good work means you keep more of your paycheck.**
- Workers always see their current rate clearly in their dashboard before they bid.

## Worked example (full transaction)
Job posted: 1 worker, 8 hrs × $25/hr = **$200 job total**
- New hirer (15%): pays **$230** total ($200 wage + $30 service fee)
- New worker (5% cut): receives **$190** ($200 − $10 platform cut)
- Top-rated worker (2%): would receive $196 for the same job
- Top-rated hirer (10%): would only pay $220

## Copy rules
- Lead with **"good work pays better"** / **"your rating sets your rate"** — that's the hook.
- Workers: never say "100% of your wage" anymore — that's outdated. Say **"keep up to 98% of your wage"** or **"the better your ratings, the more you keep."**
- Hirers: **"10–15% service fee on bookings — the better your worker reviews, the lower your fee."**
- Free to join, free to post, free to bid — **always**. The fee only happens on completed jobs.
- Never frame fees as "small" or "tiny" — just state the numbers plainly.
- New users: be upfront about the starting tier (15% hirer / 5% worker) so there are no surprises.
