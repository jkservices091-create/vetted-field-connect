## Private Submission Channel

All contributor vetting materials must be sent through the private channel below. **Do not** post references, contact information, or project photos in pull requests, issues, commit messages, or discussions.

- **Email:** jkservices091@gmail.com
- **Subject line format:** `[Vetting] <Your GitHub username> — <Repo: vetted-field-connect>`

### What to include in your vetting email
1. Your GitHub username and a link to your draft PR (if open).
2. **Three professional references**, one per job, for jobs **completed within the last 6 months**. For each reference include:
   - Reference's full name
   - Their relationship to the job (client, GC, supervisor, etc.)
   - Email address
   - Phone number
   - Job title/description, location, and start/completion dates
3. **Project photos** for each of the three jobs:
   - Minimum 3 photos per job (9 total)
   - Before/after photos where applicable
   - Each photo captioned (1–2 sentences)
   - Attach as JPG, PNG, or HEIC — keep individual files under 10 MB. For larger sets, share a private cloud folder link (Google Drive, Dropbox, etc.) with view access for the maintainer email above.

### What happens next
1. A maintainer acknowledges receipt within **3 business days**.
2. References are contacted and photos reviewed.
3. Vetting outcome is communicated privately. Approved contributors have their draft PR marked ready for review.
4. Vetting materials are retained only as long as needed to verify the contributor and are not shared outside the maintainer team.

## Reporting Code of Conduct Violations

Send a private email to **jkservices091@gmail.com** with the subject `[Conduct]`. Reports are handled confidentially per the enforcement process in `ONBOARDING.md` §2.4.

## Reporting Security Issues

Do **not** open a public issue for security vulnerabilities. Email **jkservices091@gmail.com** with the subject `[Security]` and a clear description of the issue, reproduction steps, and any suggested mitigation. We will respond within 3 business days.

## Becoming a Maintainer

Maintainership is offered by invitation after a sustained record of high-quality contributions, good judgment in code review, and adherence to the Code of Conduct. There is no application process — express interest by being a great contributor.
